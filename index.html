About Mysore

Mysore, officially known as Mysuru, is a city in the southern part of Karnataka, India. It served as the capital of the Kingdom of Mysore for nearly six centuries.

The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, magnificent palaces, beautiful gardens, and silk sarees. Mysore is also famous for its Dasara festival, which attracts tourists from all over the world.

With its pleasant climate, historical significance, and architectural marvels, Mysore offers a perfect blend of tradition and modernity, making it one of India's top tourist destinations.

Explore the must-visit places in Mysore

Mysore Palace

The official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the most famous tourist attraction in Mysore. The palace is illuminated on Sundays and during festivals.

Chamundi Hills

A prominent landmark with the Chamundeshwari Temple atop. The hill offers panoramic views of Mysore city and has a giant Nandi statue on the way.

Brindavan Gardens

Famous for its symmetric design and musical fountain shows in the evening. The gardens are located below the Krishnarajasagara dam.

St. Philomena's Church

One of the tallest churches in Asia, built in neo-gothic style. The church is dedicated to St. Philomena, a 3rd-century saint from Greece.

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical palace and the royal residence at Mysore in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore. The palace is in the centre of Mysore, and faces the Chamundi Hills
